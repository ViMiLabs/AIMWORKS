Scope and FAQ

A concise explanation of H2KG scope, non-scope, and common release questions.

In scope

  • PEMFC cathode catalyst-layer experiments, materials, processing, measurements, and provenance-rich data structures.
  • Conservative alignment of generic scientific and electrochemistry concepts to EMMO, ECHO, QUDT, ChEBI, PROV-O, and Dublin Core Terms.
  • FAIR publication support with stable IRIs, release artifacts, validation reports, and machine-readable serializations.

Out of scope

  • A general hydrogen economy ontology or broad energy-system ontology.
  • Full PEMWE, SOFC, or generic fuel-cell lifecycle modeling outside the catalyst-layer experiment scope.
  • Publishing transient measurement records or generated data nodes as part of the core asserted schema.

Frequently asked questions

Is H2KG a general hydrogen-economy ontology?

No. H2KG is a focused application ontology for PEMFC catalyst-layer experiments, materials, measurements, and provenance-aware resources.

Why are schema, controlled vocabulary, and examples published separately?

The source ontology mixes core terms with many named resources and data-like content. The release split keeps the core ontology stable and reviewable while still preserving curated reference terms and example material.

Does H2KG replace EMMO, ECHO, QUDT, or ChEBI?

No. H2KG reuses and aligns to those ontologies. Local terms are preserved for PEMFC catalyst-layer concepts that are not safely covered elsewhere.

Are mappings automatically asserted as equivalence?

No. The release pipeline is conservative and prefers subclassing or SKOS mappings unless the evidence for equivalence is strong.
